# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Employees use Onyx throughout the workday to find company knowledge, run guided AI workflows, create durable artifacts, and collaborate in shared spaces. Administrators and curators configure organization-wide knowledge, permissions, skills, memory policy, and branding. Users need fast, trustworthy access to governed capabilities without learning the underlying connector, model, or scheduling architecture.

## Product Purpose

Onyx is a company AI work surface grounded in an organization's indexed knowledge and permissions. Success means users can move from a question to cited evidence, reusable capability, scheduled workflow, or shareable work product while administrators retain clear ownership and governance. Product additions should deepen those existing Onyx domains instead of duplicating administrative surfaces or presenting disconnected feature galleries.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, capable, and trustworthy. The interface should feel work-focused and familiar while retaining Onyx's identity through its existing type, icon, color, and interaction systems.

## Anti-references

- Do not copy Perplexity's brand, visual styling, decorative carousels, or consumer-product language.
- Do not duplicate connector administration inside the user-facing Customize area.
- Do not use glass-heavy, card-within-card, oversized, or marketing-style layouts in operational surfaces.
- Do not ship static facsimiles that imply persistence, collaboration, review, or execution without implementing those contracts.

## Design Principles

1. Preserve one clear owner for each domain: Admin configures connectors and policy; Customize manages user-facing skills, workflows, and memory.
2. Put durable state behind visible controls so pinning, editing, history, sharing, and enablement survive sessions and devices.
3. Use progressive disclosure for dense catalogs: search and scope first, details and advanced controls on demand.
4. Keep evidence, ownership, status, and permissions visible wherever they affect a user's decision.
5. Extend established Onyx workflows and Opal components before introducing new interaction vocabulary.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. All actions require keyboard and visible focus support, text and state indicators must meet contrast requirements, color cannot be the only status signal, layouts must remain usable at narrow mobile widths and browser zoom, and motion must respect reduced-motion preferences.
